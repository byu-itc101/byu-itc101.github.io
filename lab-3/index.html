Lab 3 – Security Controls

Submission template: assets/Lab3.docx

Purpose

As an IT&C student, gaining a deep understanding of security controls is essential, as they form the foundation of protecting information systems. These controls—ranging from administrative policies to advanced technical implementations—will be tools you rely on extensively during your time at BYU and throughout your career in the cybersecurity field.

This lab is designed to introduce you to the principles and application of security controls through an interactive Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) tutorial and lab. In this exercise, you will analyze a simulated environment to identify and evaluate implemented security controls. By investigating logs, alerts, and system configurations, you will gain practical experience in detecting, responding to, and mitigating security threats. You may not understand all of the language and terminology found in the simulation.

Objective

  • Before beginning review the security controls found at https://www.cisecurity.org/controls/cis-controls-list
  • You will complete the SIEM simulation, at the end take a screenshot of the congratulations page to record completion.
  • After completing the simulation answer the questions listed at the bottom of the lab file.

This lab is to introduce you to the kinds of risks, threats, and associated alerts you might experience as a defender and the security controls that you might implement as protections.

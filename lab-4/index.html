Lab 4 – Information Operations Triage

Group Lab!

Submission template: assets/Lab4.docx

Docket X: assets/Docket-X.zip

Docket Y: assets/Docket-Y.zip

Purpose

Practice rapid information-triage under pressure: Act as the Rapid Credibility Assessment Cell (RCA) and triage artifacts to keep BYU users safe from weaponized information.

Your Team’s Role

You are the Rapid Credibility Assessment Cell (RCA) supporting BYU OIT. Your job is to quickly triage incoming artifacts, identify intent and likely impact, recommend first verification steps, and decide whether to dismiss, monitor, respond, or escalate. This lab has two scenarios for you to choose between. Choose ONE docket to work on, along with up to TWO more group members.

Mission Objectives

  • Label each artifact as True/Verified, Misinformation, Disinformation, Satire, or Unclear.
  • Infer the likely goal behind each artifact.
  • Note the influence techniques (TTPs) and red flags present.
  • Pick the safest first verification step for each artifact.
  • Assign a disposition (Dismiss, Monitor, Escalate, Respond).
  • State your confidence level.
  • Build a short leadership brief covering top risks and user guidance.

Rules of Engagement

  • Treat all artifacts as sensitive; do not share outside the scenario.
  • Assume screenshots can be edited, cropped, or out-of-context.
  • You are not expected to “know the truth” instantly.
  • Focus on process and reasoning—not guessing.

Assignment Workflow

  1. Open your assigned scenario packet (X or Y) and read the situation and known facts.
  2. Use the artifacts A1–A10 in your docket. For each artifact, complete the worksheet menus: category, goal, TTPs, first verification step, and disposition with confidence.
  3. Complete required detail cards in the worksheet (deeper analysis) using scenario evidence.
  4. Produce a short leadership brief: top 3 risks, a 2–3 sentence user advisory, and cyber-connection bullets.

Deliverables

  • Completed worksheet with triage decisions for A1–A10 (category, goal, TTPs, verification step, disposition, confidence).
  • Detail cards for required artifacts.
  • Short leadership brief: top 3 risks, 2–3 sentence user advisory, and cyber-connection bullets.
